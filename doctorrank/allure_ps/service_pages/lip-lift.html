Lip Lift Techniques

Dr. Heller has performed every type of lip lift, ensuring your chosen method enhances your facial balance without looking overdone.

Bullhorn (Subnasal) Lip Lift

The most popular technique in NYC. An incision is hidden just under the nose in the shape of a tiny bull's horn. This elevates the upper lip to reveal a broader smile and enhances the Cupid's bow.

Key Benefits

  • Well-concealed scar
  • Natural-looking results
  • Enhanced Cupid's bow
  • Broader smile

Direct (Gull-Wing) Lip Lift

A curved, M-shaped strip is removed along the lip line. This shortens the vertical distance above the lip and sharpens the Cupid's bow.

Key Benefits

  • Direct lift effect
  • Sharp Cupid's bow
  • Precise control
  • Immediate results

Corner Lip Lift

Small incisions at each corner lift and re-suture the skin, creating a more upturned, cheerful mouth for those with downturned corners.

Key Benefits

  • Upturned corners
  • Cheerful expression
  • Minimal scarring
  • Quick procedure

The Lip Lift Procedure

Dr. Heller typically performs the lip lift under local anesthesia in an outpatient setting. The procedure takes around one hour from start to finish.

Step 1

Preparation

During consultation, Dr. Heller marks the incision line to plan symmetry and balance. You may be advised to stop certain medications before surgery to optimize healing.

Step 2

Anesthesia

Local anesthetic is injected into the upper lip and nose area. This numbs the skin so you feel no pain during the lift.

Step 3

Incision and Lift

Dr. Heller makes a small incision along the base of the nose, gently removes a thin strip of skin, and repositions the lip upward for a fuller appearance.

Step 4

Closure

The incision is closed with fine sutures placed to minimize visible scarring. Sutures are typically removed about one week later.

Lip Lift FAQs

How long do lip lift results last?

The change is permanent. Unlike fillers that fade in months, a surgical lip lift is a one-time procedure. However, normal aging will continue, so overall facial aging still applies. Dr. Elliot Heller explains that lip lift results are permanent, emphasizing this as a lasting solution.

Is a lip lift painful?

No. Only local anesthesia is used, and most patients feel only minor discomfort. After the procedure, gentle pain meds are usually all that's needed. Dr. Elliot Heller says, 'The entire process is not considered overly painful by most patients.' Any soreness typically fades within a few days.

How visible is the scar?

Scarring is minimal. Incisions are placed where natural creases are, like right under the nose or at the corners of the mouth. With proper healing, the tiny scar is virtually hidden. Dr. Heller notes that scars from a lip lift are well-hidden under the nose and fade significantly over time.

Can lip fillers replace a lip lift?

Lip fillers add volume, but they can't reposition the lip. According to Dr. Elliot Heller, fillers are ideal for simply plumping thin lips, while a lip lift modifies the distance between the upper lip and the nose for a lifted effect. Only a lip lift permanently reshapes it and shows more tooth.

Who is a good candidate for a lip lift?

Good candidates include non-smokers in overall good health who have a longer upper lip or a thin, flat lip that hides teeth. It's important to have realistic goals and be free of medical issues that affect healing. Dr. Heller will confirm if a lip lift is right for you during consultation.

Can I combine it with other procedures?

Yes. In practice, a lip lift can be paired with other facial rejuvenation procedures for a complete refresh. Dr. Heller often combines lip lifts with facelifts, brow lifts, eyelid surgery, or non-surgical treatments like laser resurfacing and Botox for an overall younger look.
